This is what coaching actually is.

Coaching isn't therapy. It's not consulting. It's not someone telling you what to do. It's a structured partnership built around you — your situation, your goals, your obstacles — with someone who asks the right questions and holds the space for you to find your own answers.

I work with professionals who've hit a ceiling and aren't sure why, with creators trying to bring something into the world without losing their minds in the process, and with people who've been laid off and need more than a resume refresh — they need to figure out what actually fits.

What my clients share: they're capable, they're motivated, and something is in the way that they can't quite see clearly on their own. That's exactly where coaching works.

I bring decades of experience coaching individuals and teams through real change — in tech organizations, in schools, in startups — using tools from Human Systems Dynamics, Design Thinking, and Systems Thinking. I'm trained at the Hudson Institute of Coaching, one of the most respected coaching programs in the world.

The first conversation is free. There's no pressure and no commitment. If it's not the right fit, I'll tell you — and I may even know someone who is.
